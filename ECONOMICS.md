# Unit Economics

**What is a converted lead worth to Credex?**
If a startup is overspending by $1,000/mo and Credex offers a 20% discount through resold credits, Credex captures a spread (e.g., buying at 40% discount, selling at 20% discount).
- Spread = 20% of $1,000 = $200/mo gross margin.
- LTV (Lifetime Value) = Assuming 12 months retention = $2,400 per converted lead.

**CAC at each channel:**
- **Cold Email:** $0 ad spend. Cost is labor/tooling (Apollo/Instantly) ≈ $50/month. Yields 5 leads. CAC = $10.
- **HN / Organic:** $0 ad spend. CAC = $0 (pure sweat equity).
- **VC Portfolio Intro:** $0 ad spend. Highest converting channel. CAC = $0.

**Conversion Math to Profitability:**
Assuming fixed operational costs of the tool (Vercel, Supabase, Anthropic API) ≈ $50/mo.
- 1,000 Visitors
- 150 Audits Completed (15%)
- 15 High-Savings Leads captured (10%)
- 3 Credex Consultations Booked (20%)
- 1 Credit Purchase (33% close rate).
One purchase covers the entire month's operational cost 40x over ($2,400 LTV vs $50 cost). Highly profitable.

**Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months:**
To generate $1M ARR at a $2,400 LTV ($200/mo), we need ~417 active customers.
Working backwards from the funnel:
- 417 Customers
- 1,250 Consultations Booked (33% close rate)
- 6,250 High-Savings Leads (20% book rate)
- 62,500 Audits Completed (10% high-savings)
- ~400,000 Visitors over 18 months (~22,000/month).

**What has to be true?**
1. The tool must go viral within engineering communities. Shareable URLs must be highly utilized to drive organic referral loops (a CTO shares their audit result to their CFO).
2. The supply side of Credex (discounted credits) must scale to meet the demand of 400+ new startups without inventory drying up.
